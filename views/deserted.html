Deserted

Overview

Deserted is a first person survival shooter game. It consists of two levels, one is a shooting range which introduces the player to the basic mechanics of the game, such as movement, sprinting, crouching, shooting, reloading, etc. The second level is a zombie survival level, where the player has more freedom to move around the map while fighting off hordes of zombies. The player will gain points as they fight for survival and can spend those points to purcahse other weapons and/or replenish ammo.

Tools used

Unreal Engine 5, Blueprints, Animation Blueprints, Blender

Role

- Programmed player controls with the Enhanced Input Subsystem, such as movement, sprinting, jumping, crouching, shooting, aim down sight (ADS), reloading, switching weapons, picking up weapons, dropping weapons
- Implemented smooth transition logic between different player control states
- Developed a weapon struct system using Object-Oriented Programming, allowing for various different types of weapons
- Implemented different gun fire modes (automatic fire, semi-automatic fire, and burst fire) allowing the player to switch between different fire modes on various weapons, offering more variety in gameplay
- Programmed a target dummy actor that is used in the shooting range level and logic between hit detection and resetting the target dummy
- Programmed the logic behind a time trial feature in the shooting range level through blueprint gamemode

Role (Cont.)

- Implemented a weapon buy system similiar to that of Call of Duty Zombies mode, where the player gains points while fighting the zombie horde and can spend those points to purchase various weapons off the wall or replenish ammo
- Programmed the logic for transitioning between zombie waves through blueprint gamemode
- Implemented different zombie types using inheritance, allowing for different zombie attacks and stats
- Programmed enemy AI behavior logic, such as simple autonomous behavior (seeking when the player is in range or free roaming)
- Developed player health system, syncing update logic with widget blueprints to display visuals and current health or game over (death) screen
- Implemented toggleable game settings, such as mouse sensitivity, hold to crouch, hold to sprint features, that the player can adjust to better fit their gameplay preferences
- Programmed the game states and transition logic between the different game states and levels
- Designed the level layout of the shooting range and zombie levels

Platform

Windows

Duration

Less than 8 weeks

Team size
